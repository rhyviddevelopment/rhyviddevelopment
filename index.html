About Me

I design with intent.
I build with precision.

I'm Ibukunoluwa Oyekunle, a UI/UX Designer and developer based in Lagos. I sit at the intersection of design and engineering — which means I don't just design how something looks, I think about how it works, how it's built, and how real people will use it.

My work spans visual design, full-stack web development, and enterprise software configuration. Whether I'm crafting a brand identity, designing a mobile app, building a Next.js product from scratch, or developing custom Odoo modules for a production business — I bring the same standard to every layer of the work.

I'm driven by the gap between how things look and how they actually function. My goal is always to close it.

How I Work

The process
behind the work

Every project I take on follows the same four-phase discipline — understand the problem deeply, design a solution that fits it, build it to the standard it deserves, and refine until it's right.

01

Discover

I start by understanding the problem, not the solution. User research, stakeholder context, business constraints, and technical environment — everything that shapes what the right answer actually is.

02

Design

Wireframes to high-fidelity in Figma, informed by the research. I design systems, not screens — components, states, edge cases, and the logic that holds it all together.

03

Build

Where needed, I build. Next.js, Supabase, Odoo — I implement the designs in production-grade code, which means every decision I make in Figma is one I could build myself.

04

Refine

Real users surface what prototypes hide. I test, iterate, and fix — not as a formality, but because the gap between a good design and a great product is always closed in this phase.
