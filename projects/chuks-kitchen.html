Project Overview

Replacing WhatsApp orders with a real app

Chuks Kitchen is a food business run by Mr. Chukwudi Okorie — serving authentic Nigerian meals to busy professionals, students, and families. The brand is friendly, modern, and energetic. But the ordering process wasn't any of those things.

Customers were placing orders through WhatsApp messages and phone calls. There was no structured menu, no consistent user journey, and no way to scale. The client needed a proper digital ordering experience — one that matched the energy of the brand and let customers order quickly, confidently, and independently.

I was brought in to design the full mobile app UI/UX end-to-end: from defining the user flow through to a high-fidelity prototype and a design system — working with a BRD that left several areas open to designer judgment.

My Role

One designer.
Every decision.

This project came with a BRD from True Mind Innovations' business analysis team — but several areas were intentionally left open for designer judgment. Authentication method, payment UI, error states, and the onboarding flow all required me to make and document my own decisions.

As the sole designer, I owned everything from the user flow definition through to the final prototype. No handoffs, no waiting — just clear problem-solving within a tight scope.

Responsibilities

  • Analysed the BRD and identified 5 open UX gaps requiring designer assumptions — onboarding, authentication, payment UI, error states, and re-order flow
  • Defined the complete end-to-end user flow: app open → home → menu → product detail → cart → checkout → order confirmation
  • Mapped three distinct user types (busy professionals, students, families) and designed for their specific needs — speed, affordability, and ease respectively
  • Created lo-fi wireframes for all 9 core screens to validate structure and navigation hierarchy before any visual work
  • Built a Design System Lite covering colours, typography, and UI components consistent with the brand guidelines
  • Delivered 10+ high-fidelity screens and an interactive Figma prototype covering the full primary user flow

Design Process

How the work happened

01

Discover

Reviewed the BRD thoroughly, identifying known gaps and open questions. Mapped the three target user types — busy professionals, students, and families — and studied competitor apps to understand ordering patterns in the Nigerian market.

02

Define

Translated the BRD's functional requirements (FR-01 through FR-06) into a concrete user flow. Documented design assumptions for all 5 open gaps — including choosing email-based auth and a standard card/transfer payment UI.

03

Wireframe

Built lo-fi wireframes for all 9 core screens — focusing on clear navigation hierarchy, readable food categories, and minimal-friction cart management. Structure was validated before any colour or typography decisions were made.

04

Design & Deliver

Applied the brand guidelines (orange, pink, blue — friendly, modern, energetic) across a Design System Lite, then designed all 10+ hi-fi screens. Delivered a complete interactive prototype covering the full primary user flow.

Design Decisions

Assumptions made, documented

Onboarding

Designed a splash-to-home onboarding flow with a strong food hero image and a single CTA. Assumed first-time users need immediate visual appetite appeal, not a multi-step walkthrough.

Authentication

Chose email and password as the primary auth method, with a guest checkout option. Phone number login was not finalised in the BRD — email was selected for wider compatibility.

Payment UI

Designed three payment options: card, bank transfer, and pay on delivery. Payment provider was not specified in the BRD — the UI was built to be provider-agnostic.

Error & Edge Cases

Designed an empty cart state as a key edge case screen. Failed payment, network error, and re-order flows were scoped out — flagged as items for Phase 2.
