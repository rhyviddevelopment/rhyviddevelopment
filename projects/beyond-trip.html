Project Overview

A travel blog built for wanderers

BeyondTrip is a travel blog platform with a mission to bring available trips, vacations, tours and dream destinations to a global audience. With a team of 38 like-minded individuals, the platform aims to help users create lifetime memories and make destination dreams come true.

The design challenge was to create an experience that made vast travel content feel organised and discoverable — letting users browse destinations, read about places, and find their next adventure without friction. The platform needed to feel inspiring, not overwhelming.

This was an early career project from 2021 — a full end-to-end web UI design covering the home page, explore section, places directory, blog, and about page, from lo-fi wireframes through to high-fidelity screens.

Design Process

Structure first,
visuals second

01

Define the Pages

Mapped out the full site architecture: Home, Explore, Places, Blog, About. Each page had a distinct job — Home for inspiration, Places for discovery, About for trust-building.

02

Lo-Fi Wireframes

Built grayscale wireframes for every page at both desktop and mobile breakpoints. Focused on content hierarchy, navigation structure, and key interaction patterns before any colour or imagery was introduced.

03

Hi-Fi Design

Applied the teal and yellow brand palette, real travel photography, and a clean typographic system to bring the wireframes to life. Designed responsively across desktop and mobile views.

Design Decisions

Key choices & rationale

Colour

Teal + Yellow

Deep teal was chosen as the primary brand colour — it evokes ocean, nature, and calm adventure. The warm yellow accent (used on the sun in the logo and CTA buttons) adds energy and optimism. Together they communicate exploration without aggression.

Navigation

5-Page Structure

Home, Explore, Places, Blog, and About were chosen as the five top-level pages — each with a distinct content purpose. The Places page became the discovery hub, with destination cards showing location, price, rating, and booking categories (Flight, Taxi, Food, Hotel).

Hero Design

Quote-Led Homepage

The home page hero leads with the J.R.R. Tolkien quote "Not All Those Who Wander Are Lost" — immediately establishing the emotional tone of the brand. A full-bleed travel photograph with a teal overlay and a prominent search bar turns inspiration into action in a single scroll.

Wireframe Approach

Structure Before Style

Lo-fi wireframes were completed for all pages at both desktop and mobile before any colour or visual design began. This ensured layout hierarchy and content structure were validated independently of aesthetics — a discipline that influenced all later work.

Responsiveness

Mobile-First Thinking

Wireframes were created at both desktop and mobile breakpoints simultaneously. The Places page mobile wireframe used a stacked card layout with clear pricing, ratings, and category icons — making destination browsing as easy on a phone as on a laptop.

Trust Building

About + Newsletter

The About page introduced BeyondTrip as a team of 38 like-minded individuals — humanising the platform. A newsletter sign-up section with privacy policy consent was placed immediately below, converting trust into subscription.

Looking Back

What this project
taught

Every designer has a project that shaped how they think. Beyond Trip was that project — it established habits and principles that still show up in work done today.

  • Wireframes are not a formality — they are the most important design decision
  • Navigation structure determines how users feel about a product before they see a single colour
  • Designing for two breakpoints simultaneously builds better spatial instincts
  • Emotional resonance (the Tolkien quote, the photography) is a design tool, not decoration
  • A newsletter sign-up is only as good as the trust built on the page above it
  • Early work done with discipline becomes the foundation for everything that follows
