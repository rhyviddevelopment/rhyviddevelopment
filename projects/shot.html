Project Overview

Photography that captures essence

Shot is a photography studio built on a single belief: great photography goes beyond snapping a picture. It tells a story, evokes emotion, and captures the essence of the world around you. The brand needed a web presence that embodied exactly that — visually arresting, emotionally immediate, and unmistakably distinct.

The homepage design centres on a bold geometric hero: a graduated circular crop of a portrait subject against a vivid crimson circle, overlaid on a clean white canvas with soft decorative spheres and flowing topographic line art. The result is a composition that feels curated and cinematic — less like a website template and more like an editorial spread.

The design system — a crisp black-and-white base activated by a singular crimson red — ensures that every element serves the photography. The brand never competes with the images; it frames them.

Logo Design

The mark that says everything

SH◎T

The Wordmark

The logo replaces the letter O in SHOT with a camera aperture symbol ◎ — a concentric circle that references both the lens and the shutter. It is typographically seamless: the aperture sits at the same x-height as the surrounding letters, so the mark reads as a word before it reads as a symbol.

◎

The Symbol

Isolated, the aperture ring becomes the brand icon — usable as a favicon, watermark, or social avatar. Its concentric ring structure mirrors the circular crops used throughout the hero layout, creating a visual system where the logo and the layout reinforce each other.

The Tagline

The hero headline uses selective crimson weight on the words CAPTURE and ESSENCE — the two verbs that define the brand's promise. Black anchors the sentence structure; red highlights the emotional payload. It's a typographic technique that functions like a focal point in a photograph.

Design Decisions

Built like a photograph

Every decision in the Shot homepage was made the way a photographer frames a shot — with intention, restraint, and a clear subject. The design never decorates for decoration's sake. Each element has a compositional role: the red circle is the background plane, the portrait is the subject, the topographic lines are the ground texture, and the soft spheres are the bokeh.

The challenge was to create a homepage that felt instantly impressive without relying on a full-bleed dark hero or heavy photography. The white background was a deliberate constraint — forcing all visual impact to come from composition and colour rather than atmosphere.

White Background Hero

Most photography websites go dark to let images dominate. Shot goes white — a confident choice that makes the crimson circle and portrait pop with maximum contrast, and signals that the brand's graphic identity is as strong as its photography.

Circular Portrait Crop

The subject is cropped into a circle against a solid red disc. This references the camera lens directly — you are looking through an aperture at the subject — and creates a focal point that immediately draws the eye to the centre of the composition.

Topographic Line Art

Flowing contour lines in muted crimson and blue fill the lower portion of the hero, adding depth and movement without introducing new colours. They echo the organic curves of the portrait and give the white space a texture without cluttering it.

Minimal Navigation

Three links — About, Portfolio, Services — plus a prominent "Contact us" CTA button in solid red. The nav is light enough to disappear into the page on scroll, keeping the hero composition uninterrupted for as long as possible.

Design System

Colour, type & geometry

Colour Palette

#B8002A Crimson
#0A0A0A Near Black
#FFFFFF White
#C8E8E0 Soft Mint

Crimson (#B8002A) — The singular accent colour. Used with total restraint — the circular background shape, the CTA button, the logo aperture, the headline keywords. Because it appears rarely, it hits hard every time. This is the colour of urgency, passion, and the red-eye flash.

Near Black & White — The base system. Clean, high-contrast, editorial. A photography brand should never compete with its own images; the neutral base ensures every photograph shown on the site holds maximum visual weight.

Soft Mint (#C8E8E0) — A single decorative accent sphere in the hero adds a breath of cool air against the warm red circle. It prevents the composition from feeling monochromatic while remaining firmly in the background.
